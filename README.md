# E-commerce Data Analysis using SQL

## Project Overview
This project analyzes an E-commerce dataset using SQL to generate meaningful business insights related to customers, orders, products, and payments.

The analysis focuses on identifying customer behavior, sales performance, revenue insights, and payment issues.

## Database Tables
The project uses the following tables:

- Customers
- Orders
- Order_Items
- Products
- Payments

## Key Business Questions Solved

1. Find customers who placed more than 3 orders
2. Identify top 5 customers based on total purchase value
3. Find products that were never ordered
4. Identify the most sold products
5. Retrieve each customer’s latest order
6. Detect orders with failed payments but marked as delivered
7. Calculate revenue generated by product category
8. Identify customers with orders but no successful payments
9. Calculate total order bill amount after discount
10. Rank customers by total spending within each city
11. Find second highest priced product in each category
12. Month-wise total orders and revenue analysis

## SQL Concepts Used

- Joins
- Aggregate Functions
- Subqueries
- Window Functions
- Group By & Having
- Ranking Functions
- Business Logic Analysis

## Tools Used

- MySQL
- SQL
- GitHub

# Key Insights from the Analysis

High-Value Customers Identified:
A small group of customers placed more than 3 orders, indicating a loyal customer base that contributes significantly to overall sales.

Top Revenue Generating Customers:
The top 5 customers contribute a large share of the total purchase value, showing that revenue is concentrated among a few high-spending customers.

Products with Zero Demand:
Some products have never been ordered, indicating poor demand or lack of visibility on the platform.

Best Selling Products:
Certain products have significantly higher total quantity sold, making them key drivers of sales performance.

Customer Purchase Behavior:
Each customer's latest order activity helps identify recently active customers and potential repeat buyers.

Payment System Issues:
Some orders are marked Delivered even when the payment failed, which indicates possible system or operational errors.

Revenue Distribution by Category:
Some product categories generate higher revenue than others, showing which product segments drive the business.

Unsuccessful Payment Customers:
Some customers placed orders but never completed successful payments, suggesting checkout or payment friction.

Order Billing Analysis:
The calculation of order bill amounts after discounts helps understand the real revenue generated after promotional offers.

City-Level Customer Spending:
Customer spending varies significantly across cities, with some cities having higher purchasing power.

Product Pricing Strategy:
Finding the second highest priced product per category helps understand pricing distribution within product categories.

Monthly Sales Trends:
Month-wise order and revenue analysis reveals seasonal sales patterns and business growth trends.

# Business Recommendations

Focus on High-Value Customers:
Create loyalty programs, exclusive discounts, or reward points to retain top spending customers.

Promote Best-Selling Products:
Highlight the most sold products on the homepage to further increase conversions.

Improve Visibility of Unordered Products:
Products with zero orders should be promoted through discounts, ads, or better product descriptions.

Fix Payment Processing Issues:
Orders marked delivered with failed payments should be investigated to prevent financial loss.

Optimize Product Categories:
Invest more in categories generating the highest revenue while reviewing low-performing categories.

Reduce Payment Drop-offs:
Improve payment gateway reliability and simplify checkout to reduce failed transactions.

Target High-Spending Cities:
Run location-based marketing campaigns in cities with higher customer spending.

Analyze Seasonal Trends:
Use month-wise revenue trends to plan promotions, inventory, and marketing strategies.
